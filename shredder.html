THE COLUMBUS TIMES

"Just a kid from Whitehall"

I was born in Columbus, Ohio. I have lived here most of my life except for when I had briefly served in the U.S. Military. I am a husband and father of 2 young boys.

My wife and I met back in late 2014 just around Christmas when we met mutually through friends at Lazer Kraze in Lewis Center, Ohio. Not much longer after we met, I proposed to her on our trip to myrtle beach. After some apartment living for awhile, we got married in June of 2018 and purchased our first home in Canal Winchester and welcomed our first son into the world, Wyatt Fuller.

My first job doing something somewhat techy, I was working at AT&T where I would troubleshoot user phone issues, setting up new devices, and addressing billing issues.

That's when I realized I could do something in the tech space, I decided to look for full-time work while I was pursuing my degree and somehow fell into a full-time IT role on the service desk at Safelite Autoglass.

After some time, I became their marketing and promotions manager for match presentation. Some notable achievements for me in this role was pioneering the largest drone show in the history of Columbus.

Another is leading the pack on the goal song campaign which received publicity throughout the city through various news outlets and discussed on 97.1 the fan.

I finished up my degree while working full-time and unsure of whether or not I was going to land a role in sports, I decided to pursue my masters. Midway through that program, I was hired by the Columbus Crew as their IT manager.

After my stint in marketing, nights and weekends were no longer my ideal lifestyle so I chose to pursue balance. Shortly after leaving the crew, our 2nd son was born into the world. Welcome to the world Theodore Ray Fuller.
